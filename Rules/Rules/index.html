Rules and Regulations

Eligibility :

  • Participants: Open to individuals or teams. Teams can consist of 2-5 members.
  • Age Requirement: Participants must be at least 18 years old.
  • Pre-requisites: Participants should have basic programming knowledge.

Registration :

  • Registration Period: All participants must register before the deadline specified on the event page.
  • Team Formation: Teams can be formed before the event or on the day of the hackathon.

Project Requirements :

  • Originality: Projects must be original and developed during the hackathon. Reusing code is allowed if properly credited, but the core of the project must be unique.
  • Submission: All code must be submitted via the designated platform (e.g., GitHub) by the deadline.
  • Languages & Tools: Participants can use any programming language or development tool they prefer.
  • Open Source: Participants are encouraged to use open-source libraries and tools, but they must be credited properly.

Conduct :

  • Collaboration: Collaboration between teams is not allowed. Participants must work within their own team..
  • Behavior: All participants must adhere to the event’s code of conduct. Harassment, discrimination, and inappropriate behavior will not be tolerated.
  • Judging Criteria: Projects will be judged on creativity, technical difficulty, design, and usefulness. The judges' decision is final.

Event Format :

  • Kick-Off: The hackathon will begin with a briefing where the theme or challenge will be announced.
  • Working Period: Teams will have a set period (e.g., 24 or 48 hours) to develop their projects
  • Check-Ins: Optional or mandatory check-ins may be scheduled to monitor progress.
  • Final Submission: Projects must be submitted before the final deadline.

Prizes :

  • Award Categories: Prizes will be awarded in different categories, such as Best Overall, Most Innovative, Best Design, etc..
  • Disqualification: Teams may be disqualified for violating any rules or engaging in unethical behavior.

Post-Hackathon :

  • Demos: Selected teams may be required to demo their projects.
  • Feedback: Participants are encouraged to provide feedback on the event.
  • Networking: After the hackathon, there may be opportunities for networking and collaboration.
